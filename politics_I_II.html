Politics Book I establishes the city as a natural community — the completion of the household and village, prior to them in the order of nature though posterior in the order of generation. It analyses the household's internal relations and gives the famous account of man as a political animal. Book II examines proposed ideal states and property arrangements, giving Aristotle's critique of Plato's Republic and Laws and of the proposals of Phaleas and Hippodamus.
