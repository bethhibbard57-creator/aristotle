Metaphysics Δ (Delta) · Ε (Epsilon) · Ζ (Zeta) · Η (Eta)

The determination of being: the philosophical lexicon, the kinds of being, being as substance, matter and form

The second tetralemmic position — the terms determined. These four books establish the conceptual vocabulary and the positive account of what being is. Delta clears the ground by distinguishing senses of key terms. Epsilon maps the field. Zeta and Eta are the sustained analysis of being as substance, culminating in the identification of being with actuality.

Click any chapter to expand. Position II is the conceptual heart of the Metaphysics. Delta (30 chapters) is the philosophical dictionary of the Metaphysics — every key term distinguished in its multiple senses. Epsilon identifies which kind of being is primary. Zeta (the longest and densest book) investigates what being is — working through matter, essence, universal, genus, and substrate to arrive at form as the answer. Eta draws out the consequences: being is form, form is actuality, and the unity of a defined thing is explained by the relation of actuality to potentiality.
