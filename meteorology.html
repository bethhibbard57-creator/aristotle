The sublunary region between the heavens and the earth — weather, the sea, earthquakes, minerals, and the properties of homoeomerous bodies

Click any section to expand. The Meteorology occupies the middle position in the natural philosophy — between the celestial (On the Heavens) and the terrestrial (GC, biological works). It applies the four-cause framework and the elemental analysis of GC to the phenomena of the sublunary atmosphere: winds, rain, clouds, thunder, lightning, earthquakes, rivers, the sea, comets, and the Milky Way. Book IV, which analyses the properties of homoeomerous bodies, is the most philosophically significant for the thesis — it bridges the elemental analysis of GC II and the biological analysis of PA II.
