The Connections — Organised by Paper

All philosophical connections identified across the Aristotelian corpus reference library · organised by the four-paper project structure · March 2026 · companion to: The Connections — Master Summary (in philosophical order of the corpus)

The master claim: The Aristotelian corpus is a single unified argument. The same four-position tetralemmic structure appears at every level. Aristotelian logic and Buddhist epistemology are structurally identical by identity of logical form, not by analogy requiring paraconsistent bridging. Every classical logical paradox arises from improper predication — from a suppressed qualification that, once restored, dissolves the paradox. The categorical classification of the biological works provides the empirical truth conditions for the entire logical structure.
Paper One
Paradox dissolution via improper predication
Every classical logical paradox fails one of the four elenchus conditions of De Interpretatione Ch. 11. The dissolution is the secundum quid move: restoring the suppressed qualification. LNC and PEM apply only to individuals with resolved temporal identity.
Paper Two
The Organon as unified tetralemmic architecture
The Organon is not a collection but a single progressive argument. Categories Ch. 2 already contains all four tetralemma positions. The indefinite name, the elenchus conditions, and the syllogistic figures are the same structure at escalating levels of formal precision.
Paper Three
The Metaphysics as recursive tetralemmic structure
The Metaphysics is a single progressive elenctic argument in four positions. Lambda is the thesis that Mu-Nu defend by elenchus. The Unmoved Mover is structurally identical to Buddhist unconditioned and the unnameable Tao — not by analogy but by identity of logical form.
Paper Four
The complete corpus as tetralemmic structure
The same four-position structure runs through every domain of the corpus. Aristotelian logic and Buddhist epistemology are structurally identical — not requiring Eastern-Western bridging via paraconsistent logic but by structural identity of logical form.

P1 Paper One — Paradox dissolution via improper predication

Primary sources: Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 (secundum quid) · De Int. Ch. 11 (elenchus conditions) · Met. Γ.3 (LNC conditions) · Phys. VI.9 (Zeno) · NE VII.3 (weakness of will) · On Memory Ch. 1 (temporal identity) · PA I.2 (truth conditions). The method is one; the applications are many.

The four elenchus conditions and the temporal identity condition

The four conditions stated and their grounding
De Int. Ch. 11 · Met. Γ.3 · Phys. IV.11–14 · On Memory Ch. 1 · Cat. Ch. 5
A genuine contradictory pair requires: same subject · same predicate · same respect · same time. These four are identical to the conditions of LNC in Met. Γ.3. The elenchus is the dialogical form of LNC. The four conditions are grounded in: the physics of time (Phys. IV.11 — time as the number of motion with respect to before and after); the psychology of memory (On Memory Ch. 1 — the soul marks experiences as past by a temporal mark); and the ontology of primary being (Cat. Ch. 5 — the same subject receives contraries through time while persisting). LNC and PEM apply only to individuals with resolved temporal identity. Without memory there is no temporal identity; without temporal identity LNC cannot be properly applied.
The master dissolution: the secundum quid move
Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 and 23 · Phys. I.8 · Phys. VI.9 · De Int. Ch. 9 · NE VII.3
Every paradox suppresses a qualification — treating a qualified predication as absolute. Restoring the qualification dissolves it. Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 is the primary source. Applied to: the Eleatic argument against change (Phys. I.8 — privation ≠ absolute non-being); Zeno's four paradoxes (Phys. VI.9 — potential infinity ≠ actual infinity; instantaneous position ≠ rest); the Liar (Soph. Ref. Ch. 4, 10); Russell's paradox; the Sea Battle (De Int. Ch. 9); Schrödinger's Cat; the Ship of Theseus (Topics II.5, Phys. V.3); weakness of will (NE VII.3 — having ≠ using knowledge). One method; every classical paradox.

The worked paradoxes — primary sources for each

The Liar Paradox
Soph. Ref. Ch. 4 (equivocation) · Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 (secundum quid) · Cat. Ch. 1 (homonymy)
'This sentence is false' — equivocation on 'is false' across levels of application (Soph. Ref. Ch. 4) combined with the secundum quid fallacy (Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 — the qualified 'is false in this self-referential respect' treated as 'is false absolutely'). Restoring the qualification: the sentence is false-in-this-qualified-sense, not false-absolutely. The homonymy of 'is false' across different predication contexts is the Cat. Ch. 1 analysis of the same error.
Russell's Paradox
Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 · Soph. Ref. Ch. 15 (many questions as one) · Cat. Ch. 2 (the four-fold grid)
The set-of-all-sets question conflates two different predication contexts — membership-at-level-n and membership-as-such — treating them as one (many questions as one — Soph. Ref. Ch. 15). Russell's erasure of the Aristotelian distinction between individual and universal (Cat. Ch. 2's four-fold grid) removes the level-distinction that prevents the paradox. Restoring the level-distinction (which Cat. Ch. 2 already gives) dissolves it.
The Sea Battle (De Interpretatione Ch. 9)
De Int. Ch. 9 · Met. Γ.6 · Phys. IV.11–14 · Post. An. I.30
The disjunction is necessarily true; neither disjunct is yet determined. LNC and PEM apply to the disjunction (same time = now); they do not apply to individual future disjuncts whose temporal identity is not yet resolved. The future has not yet acquired the before-and-after structure of numbered time. The qualification suppressed: 'necessarily one or the other' ≠ 'each one necessarily'. PEM applies to the disjunction; it does not apply with the same force to each unresolved disjunct.
Zeno's Four Paradoxes — Physics VI.9 as primary source for Paper One
Phys. III.8 · Phys. VI.9 · Phys. VI.3 · Phys. VI.7
Four suppressions, four restorations: Dichotomy and Achilles suppress potential/actual — treat infinitely divisible (potential) as if requiring infinitely many actual steps. Arrow suppresses instant/interval — treats being-at-a-position-at-an-instant as being-at-rest-across-an-interval. The arrow is moving across the interval; the instant gives only its position, not its state. Stadium suppresses relative/absolute — treats time-to-pass-a-moving-body as equivalent to time-to-pass-a-stationary-body. All four are Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 applied to the physics of motion. Physics VI.9 is a primary source for Paper One.
The Ship of Theseus
Topics II.5 · Phys. V.3 · Cat. Ch. 5 · NE III.5 · NE VIII.13
Physics V.3 gives the precise vocabulary: continuous → contiguous → successive. The ship is the same if its parts are continuously connected through time; it is a different ship if the connection is merely successive. Cat. Ch. 5 grounds this: the same subject receives contraries through time while persisting. The problem dissolves by determining which level of continuity applies. Ethical dimensions: character as temporal identity of the moral subject (NE III.5); friendship dissolved when character changes beyond recognition (NE VIII.13).
Weakness of Will — NE VII.3 as primary source for Paper One
NE VII.3 · DA III.10 · Soph. Ref. Ch. 10
The paradox: how can someone act against what they know to be best? Dissolution: distinguish having knowledge (present but inactive) from using knowledge (actively exercised). The qualification suppressed: 'acts against knowledge' should be 'acts against knowledge-in-the-using-sense'. Passion suppresses the minor premise of the practical syllogism. Restoring the distinction dissolves the paradox — the same secondary quid move as every other dissolution. NE VII.3 is a primary source for Paper One.
Moral responsibility as proper predication — the ethical counterpart of the elenchus conditions
NE III.1 · EE II.6–7 · Soph. Ref. Ch. 11
To praise or blame is to predicate virtue or vice of an agent — but the predication is proper only when the action was voluntary. The four conditions of involuntariness (compulsion, ignorance of particulars, ignorance of circumstances, ignorance of manner) are the ethical counterparts of the four elenchus conditions. Each specifies when the predication of moral responsibility fails. The EE (II.6–7) states this more precisely than the NE.
Equity — the secundum quid dissolution in practical reasoning
NE V.10 · Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 · Rhet. I.13
The universal law is the unqualified rule; equity restores the qualification the particular case requires. The equitable person recognises that the universal does not apply without qualification to this particular case. This is the secundum quid move applied to practical reasoning — the same move that dissolves the logical paradoxes. The logical, ethical, and rhetorical analyses of the qualified universal are one analysis at three levels.
Categorical classification gives truth values — the empirical ground of the dissolution
PA I.2–5 · HA I.1 · Topics IV · Post. An. I.4
Every paradox that involves a subject whose categorical placement is ambiguous has undetermined truth conditions. The biological classification system shows what it looks like when categorical placement is determinate: per se attributes are fixed, accidental attributes are recognisable as accidental, and the conditions under which LNC applies are fully specified. The biological corpus is the empirical proof of the logical thesis. Monstrosities (GA IV.3–4) are the biological case: individuals whose actual predicates deviate from their per se predicates — what they are accidentally rather than per se.

P2 Paper Two — The Organon as unified tetralemmic architecture

Primary sources: Cat. Ch. 2 (all four positions present from the start) · De Int. Ch. 10–11 (indefinite name and elenchus) · Pr. An. I.46 (indefinite name in syllogistic) · Topics VIII (elenchus in practice) · Soph. Ref. Ch. 34 (explicit closure). The Organon is one progressive argument, not a collection.

Categories Ch. 2 — all four tetralemmic positions already present
Cat. Ch. 2 · De Int. Ch. 10 · Pr. An. I.46
The four-fold grid of Cat. Ch. 2 generates exactly four positions: primary being (not said-of, not in) · secondary being (said-of, not in) · universal accident (said-of, in) · individual accident (not said-of, in). These are the four tetralemmic positions at the categorical level. The indefinite name makes all four expressible as proposition forms (De Int. Ch. 10). Pr. An. I.46 shows the indefinite name operationally essential to valid syllogistic. The tetralemmic structure is present from the first chapter of the first work and runs continuously through the last.
The six-stage progressive argument of the Organon
Categories through Sophistical Refutations · Rhetoric as the closing loop
Categories (what is) → De Interpretatione (what is said) → Prior Analytics (argument structure) → Posterior Analytics (knowledge conditions) → Topics (dialectical argument) → Sophistical Refutations (pathologies and their dissolution). Soph. Ref. Ch. 34 explicitly closes the project — Aristotle notes nothing existed before his work on this and that it has been brought to completion. The Rhetoric closes the argumentative loop: the same syllogistic structure at the level of public persuasion, the same per se / accidental distinction in a new domain.
The elenchus as the formal core running through the entire Organon
De Int. Ch. 11 · Topics VIII · Soph. Ref. Ch. 11 and 24 · Met. Γ.3–4
The elenchus — four conditions: same subject · same predicate · same respect · same time — appears in: De Int. Ch. 11 (stated); Topics VIII (practiced in dialectical exchange); Soph. Ref. Ch. 11 (as the master fallacy ignoratio elenchi whose violation is the failure to achieve genuine contradiction); Soph. Ref. Ch. 24 (as the master dissolution); Met. Γ.3 (as the conditions of LNC). The elenchus is simultaneously a logical instrument, a dialogical practice, and an ontological principle. It is the thread that holds the Organon together.
The Socratic elenchus = Topics Book VIII = the catuṣkoṭi in dialectical practice
Topics VIII · Soph. Ref. · De Int. Ch. 11 · Plato's Euthyphro
The Socratic elenchus as practiced in the dialogues applies Topics Book VIII using the instruments of Books I–VII to problems that turn on improper predication. Every failed definition in the Platonic dialogues fails by one of the criteria catalogued in Topics VI. The elenchus is formally identical to the catuṣkoṭi: it poses a question, explores each possible answer (positions 1, 2, 3), shows each fails, and arrives at aporia — the fourth position, which is not ignorance but the recognition that the question as posed cannot be answered within the terms in which it was posed.

P3 Paper Three — The Metaphysics as recursive tetralemmic structure

Primary sources: Met. Α–Ν as a single progressive elenctic argument · Lambda.7 and Lambda.9 as the apophatic limit · Mu-Nu as the fourth position · the structural identity with DA III.5, NE X.7, and EE VIII.3.

The four tetralemmic positions of the Metaphysics as one progressive argument
Met. Α–Γ · Δ–Η · Θ–Λ · Μ–Ν
Position I (Α α Β Γ): the question posed — the history of causes showing no previous account is complete; the aporiai as the formal tetralemmic structure of the inquiry; Gamma defending LNC as the condition of inquiry itself. Position II (Δ Ε Ζ Η): the terms determined — form surviving every challenge in Zeta; actuality explaining unity in Eta. Position III (Θ Ι Κ Λ): the answer given — the priority of actuality; the Unmoved Mover as pure actuality and self-thinking thought. Position IV (Μ Ν): the competitor dissolved — the elenchus of every version of the Platonic claim. Mu-Nu have no positive content of their own; they are the dissolution that leaves Lambda standing. The four positions are mutually constitutive: none stands without the others. Mu-Nu are held together relationally — their logical force depends entirely on Positions I–III.
The four conditions of LNC = the four elenchus conditions
Met. Γ.3 · De Int. Ch. 11
Gamma.3 states LNC with four conditions identical to De Int. Ch. 11's four elenchus conditions. The elenchus is the dialogical form of LNC. LNC is not merely a logical rule but an ontological constraint on being. First philosophy reveals what the logical analysis assumed: LNC holds because being itself is structured by the same four conditions that govern genuine contradiction. The Organon and the Metaphysics are one argument at two levels of abstraction.
Lambda.7 and Lambda.9 — the apophatic limit
Met. Λ.7 · Met. Λ.9 · Phys. VIII.10 · DA III.5
Lambda.7: noēsis noēseōs — self-thinking thought. Lambda.9: the thinker and the thought are identical in the Unmoved Mover — the duality of knower and known is dissolved. This exceeds ordinary subject-predicate structure; the text cannot proceed further without distortion. Lambda.9 is the apophatic limit of the Metaphysics and of the corpus. The same structure appears in DA III.5 (active nous: separable, unmixed, impassible, immortal) and at the limit of the Physics (VIII.10: without magnitude, moves as final cause, object of desire).
The Unmoved Mover = Buddhist unconditioned = unnameable Tao — structural identity
Met. Λ.7 · DA III.5 · NE X.7 · EE VIII.3
Each tradition has independently followed the same argument to its limit and arrived at the same point: the ground of all determination that is itself not determined, characterised only by negative predicates, known only by direct contact. The Unmoved Mover (Aristotle), the unconditioned asaṅkhata (Buddhism), the unnameable Tao (Daoism) are structurally identical — not by analogy but by identity of logical form. Paraconsistent logic and dialetheism (Priest) correctly identify superposition states but misname them as true contradictions rather than incomplete predication events. The correct dissolution is the secundum quid move, not the acceptance of true contradictions.
The divine standard across the corpus — Ethics, Psychology, Metaphysics as one claim
EE VIII.3 · NE X.7–8 · DA III.5 · Met. Λ.7
EE VIII.3: whatever most promotes the contemplation of god is best — the most explicit theological statement in the corpus. NE X.7: nous is what we most are; we must immortalise ourselves as much as possible. DA III.5: the active nous is the divine element within us. Met. Λ.7: the Unmoved Mover lives the contemplative life completely and eternally. These four texts are one claim at four levels. The Buddhist arahant living in the direct presence of the unconditioned is structurally parallel: participation in what transcends the human without ceasing to be human.

P4 Paper Four — The complete corpus as tetralemmic structure

The same four-position structure runs through every domain of the corpus. The papers are not separate arguments — they are four approaches to the same argument. Paper Four assembles the complete case: the corpus is one argument; Aristotelian logic and Buddhist epistemology are structurally identical by identity of logical form.

The tetralemmic structure at every level of the corpus
Cat. Ch. 2 · GC I.2 · Physics II.3 · Met. Α–Ν · DA II.2 · NE II · Post. An. II.19 · Pol. III.6
Logical: four predicables · four proposition forms · four elenchus conditions. Physical: four kinds of change (GC I.2) · four elements (GC II.1) · four causes (Physics II.3) · four kinds of motion (Physics V.2). Metaphysical: four positions (Α–Γ, Δ–Η, Θ–Λ, Μ–Ν). Psychological: four soul-faculties (DA II.2) · four stages of epistemological ascent (Post. An. II.19). Ethical: virtues as mean between excess and deficiency (NE II) · five intellectual virtues (NE VI.3) · six constitutions organised on two axes (Pol. III.6–7). Biological: four primary qualities constituting four elements · four causes in generation. These are not analogies — they are the same structure at different levels of the real.
The great causal chain — one structure from Unmoved Mover to individual organism
Met. Λ · Phys. VIII · On the Heavens · GC II · DA · PA · HA · GA
Unmoved Mover → eternal circular motion → celestial spheres → solar oblique motion → elemental cycle → homoeomerous stuffs → organs and organisms → living things with souls → human beings with nous → the political community → the good life oriented toward the divine. Every level is connected by causal chains of motion, form, and final cause. The vertical chain from first philosophy to natural philosophy to psychology to practical philosophy to the arts is the ontological ground of the horizontal argument of each text.
Matter as the fourth position at every level
Phys. I.9 · Met. Ζ.3 · GC II.4 · DA III.4
Prime matter (Physics I.9): neither form nor privation but the ground that makes both possible — the pure potentiality for all determination. The catuṣkoṭi's fourth position is the logical image of matter. The potential nous of DA III.4 is the intellectual analogue of prime matter: both are characterised by unlimited potentiality without any determination of their own. Both find their limit in pure actuality. The physical, psychological, and logical fourth positions are structurally identical.
Constitutional change as political elemental transformation — the four causes in collective life
Pol. V.1–4 · GC II.2 · Physics II.3
Constitutional transformation follows the same pattern as elemental transformation: extreme democracy becomes tyranny as fire becomes water (replacing one principle with its contrary). The four causes operate in constitutional change exactly as in natural change. Political change is the social analogue of elemental change. The political corpus is the Physics applied to collective human life.
Recognition (ἀναγνώρισις) as the aesthetic form of the elenchus
Poetics Ch. 11, 14 · Topics VIII · De Int. Ch. 11
Tragic recognition moves from ignorance to knowledge through the internal necessity of the plot — the same logical structure as the elenchus. Catharsis is the emotional expression of this recognition. The Poetics shows the logical structure of the elenchus operating at the level of felt emotional experience. Poetry is more philosophical than history (Poetics Ch. 9) because it expresses the universal — what a certain kind of person would probably or necessarily do — rather than the accidental particular. The aesthetic, dialectical, and logical analyses are one analysis at three levels.
Buddhist vijñāna / ālayavijñāna and DA III.4–5 — structural convergence
DA III.4–5 · On Memory Ch. 1–2
The Buddhist vijñāna (consciousness as reception of form from sense-contact) corresponds to DA III.4's potential nous (reception of intelligible forms without matter). The ālayavijñāna (storehouse consciousness as the sub-personal ground) corresponds to the active nous as the underlying productive ground. Both converge on the same logical problem: what is the ground of intellectual reception that is itself not received? Structural identity, not analogy across traditions.
Parts of Animals II–IV — the most sustained demonstration that categorical classification gives truth values
PA II–IV · PA I.4 · Physics II.8–9 P1P4
Every animal part is explained by its function (final cause). The truth conditions cascade: species-form → way of life → diet → digestive structure. The same cascading determination governs every organ in every animal. The analogy principle (same function, different material realisation across genera) is the biological expression of the distinction between per se truth at the genus level and per se truth at the species level. Parts of Animals II–IV is the most sustained empirical demonstration in the corpus of the thesis that categorical classification gives truth values for the actual — showing at the most concrete anatomical level how the species-form determines which parts an animal has per se, what matter those parts require, and why they exist where they do.
Movement of Animals — the practical syllogism as the bridge between logic and biology
MA Ch. 3–4 · DA III.10 · NE III.2–3 · Physics VIII.5 P1P4
The practical syllogism (DA III.10) is the cognitive mechanism that triggers desire and produces motion. The middle term in demonstration connects subject and predicate; the minor premise in the practical syllogism connects the universal desire to the particular action. These are the same structure at two levels: logical and biological. Motion is the biological conclusion of a logical argument. The unmoved mover within the animal (the joint as fixed point) corresponds structurally to the Unmoved Mover of Physics VIII.5 — the same principle of a fixed ground for all motion, operating at the biological and cosmological levels simultaneously.
Progression of Animals — form determines truth at the most concrete anatomical level
IA Ch. 16–19 · PA I.4 · Physics II.9 P1P4
The direction in which the human knee bends is a per se attribute of the human species — determined by the species-form and the way of life it entails. 'The human knee bends backward' is per se true of humans because the human form requires forward bipedal locomotion and forward bipedal locomotion requires backward-bending knees. The truth condition is the species-form; the species-form is the final cause. This is hypothetical necessity (Physics II.9) at the most anatomically concrete level: if the form is to be achieved, the matter must be of this kind. Form determines truth. The biological corpus as a whole confirms this in every domain from blood to knee joints.
Sleep as the biological having/not-using knowledge — On Sleep confirms NE VII.3
On Sleep Ch. 1–3 · NE VII.3 · Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 P1
Sleep is the periodic suspension of the sensitive faculty — the person has the capacity for sensation but is not using it. This is the biological instantiation of the having/not-using distinction that dissolves the paradox of weakness of will (NE VII.3). The weak-willed person has knowledge but is not using it; the sleeping person has the sensitive faculty but is not using it. The biological and ethical cases are the same logical structure: the having/using qualification is the secundum quid qualification that resolves the apparent paradox in both domains.
Meteorology IV — categorical placement gives truth values from gold to blood to bone to flesh
Meteor. IV.10–12 · GC II.4–5 · PA II P1P4
Meteorology IV extends the biological thesis into geology and mineralogy. The same analytical framework (active and passive qualities, concoction, the two exhalations as material principles) governs both geological stuffs (gold, iron, stone) and biological stuffs (blood, bone, flesh). The truth conditions for predications about homoeomerous stuffs are grounded in their material constitution and productive history. Categorical placement gives truth values for the actual at every level of the natural world — from the most inorganic (mineral) to the most organic (flesh). This completes the empirical grounding of the logical thesis across the entire range of the natural world.
The corpus closes in the right silence
Cat. Ch. 1 · Meteor. IV.12 · Met. Λ.9 · NE X.7
The corpus opens with the simplest logical distinction (Cat. Ch. 1 — homonymous vs synonymous names) and closes with the analysis of homoeomerous stuffs — from gold to blood to bone — showing that categorical placement gives truth values for the actual at every level of the natural world. The Unmoved Mover, the active nous, the contemplative life, and the divine standard are present throughout as the limit toward which the argument tends. The corpus as a whole instantiates the catuṣkoṭi: three positive domains and a fourth position that is not silence through ignorance but silence through the completion of the argument. The corpus is complete.
