Nicomachean Ethics Books I & II — complete interactive reference

I: the good and happiness · II: virtue of character — its nature and acquisition

Click any chapter to expand. NE Books I–II establish the foundations of Aristotle's ethics. Book I defines the good as the end of action, identifies happiness (εὐδαιμονία) as the highest good, gives the function argument for what happiness is, and establishes the method of ethical inquiry. Book II defines virtue of character as a stable disposition toward the mean, explains how virtues are acquired through habituation, and introduces the conditions that make an action virtuous. Together they are the ground on which the entire Ethics rests.
