VII: contact of mover and moved; comparison of motions · VIII: the eternity of motion; the Unmoved Mover — the Physics and the Metaphysics converge

Click any chapter to expand. Book VII is preparatory — it establishes that every moved thing is moved by something in contact with it, and examines how motions can be compared. Book VIII is the culmination of the entire Physics: the argument from the eternity of motion to the existence of an eternal Unmoved Mover that is the first cause of all natural motion. Physics VIII and Metaphysics Lambda are the same argument from two directions — the Physics approaches it from motion, Lambda approaches it from being. They meet at the same point: pure actuality, self-thinking thought, the ground of all change.
