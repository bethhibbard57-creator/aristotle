Parts of Animals Books II–IV — complete interactive reference

The causal analysis of animal parts: uniform parts (blood, bone, flesh) · non-uniform parts (organs) · organised by the four-cause framework established in Book I

How to read this file: Parts of Animals I established the methodological principle — the final cause is primary, the formal cause determines which material parts are required (hypothetical necessity), and the four causes together give the complete explanation. Books II–IV apply this framework systematically to every major category of animal part. The focus here is on the causal and classificatory structure rather than the empirical detail — on how each part is explained by its function (final cause), which animals have it (genus-level per se attribute), and what matter it requires (hypothetical necessity). The empirical content is the matter; the causal structure is the form.
Click any section to expand. Parts of Animals II–IV constitute the most extensive application of the four-cause framework in the corpus. Every part of every animal is explained by its function — what it is for — and the matter required for that function. The analysis confirms at the empirical level what Parts of Animals I established methodologically: the final cause is the primary explanatory principle, and the categorical placement of an animal (its genus and species) determines which parts it has per se and which it lacks.
