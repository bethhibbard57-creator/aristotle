Click any chapter to expand. De Anima is where the metaphysical account of form and actuality meets the psychology of living things. Soul is the form and first actuality of a natural body having the capacity for life. The faculties of soul — nutritive, perceptual, imaginative, appetitive, locomotive, rational — form a hierarchy of increasing complexity. Book III.4–5 on nous is the summit: the passage where Aristotle identifies the agent intellect as separable, unmixed, impassible, and immortal — structurally identical to the Unmoved Mover, and the psychological ground of the catuṣkoṭi's fourth position.
