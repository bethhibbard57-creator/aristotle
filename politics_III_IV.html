Book III is the philosophical core of the Politics — it defines the citizen, classifies constitutions, analyses the claims of different groups to rule, defends the collective wisdom of the many, and examines the rule of law vs the rule of the best person. Book IV turns from the ideal to the actual — analysing what kinds of constitutions exist, which are best for most cities under most conditions, and how they should be organised. Together Books III–IV give both the normative standard and the practical analysis of political life.
