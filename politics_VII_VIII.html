VII: the best constitution — the good life, population, territory, and the ordering of the city · VIII: education in the best city

Click any chapter to expand. Books VII–VIII are the constructive culmination of the Politics. Book VII defines the best constitution by beginning from the best life — the life identified in NE X as the contemplative — and working outward to the external conditions that make it possible. Book VIII analyses education as the primary instrument for forming the citizens of the best city. Together they complete the arc from NE I's opening question (what is the good for human beings?) through the Ethics' analysis of virtue to the Politics' account of the social conditions for the good life's realisation.
