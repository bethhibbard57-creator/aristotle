I: happiness and the good — the starting point · II: virtue defined; the voluntary and involuntary

The EE is an earlier and in some respects more direct treatment of the same territory as the NE. Its differences from the NE are philosophically significant — especially EE I's more explicit grounding of ethics in the divine, EE II's sharper account of the voluntary, and EE VIII's closing account of the divine standard of the good life. Cross-references to the NE are noted throughout.

Click any chapter to expand. The Eudemian Ethics covers much of the same ground as the Nicomachean Ethics but with different emphases and a distinctive framework. EE I is more explicitly theological — it grounds the account of happiness in the divine from the outset. EE II gives a sharper and more detailed account of voluntary action and choice. The shared books (EE IV–VI = NE V–VII) are noted. EE VIII's final chapter on the divine standard is the most important for the thesis — treated in full in the EE VII–VIII file.
