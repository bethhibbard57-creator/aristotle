Subject: the nature of generation, corruption, alteration, growth, and contact — distinguished from mere change of quality or place

Click any chapter to expand. Book I establishes the fundamental distinctions among the kinds of change: generation and corruption (coming-to-be and passing-away absolutely), alteration (change of quality while the subject persists), and growth and diminution (change of quantity). It then analyses the conditions of contact, action, and passion that make change possible. Book II treats the four elements and their transformations.
