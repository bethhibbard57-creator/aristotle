The Porphyrian tree made flesh — the genus-species-differentia classification of the animal kingdom as the empirical grounding of the logical apparatus

Click any book to expand. The History of Animals is the largest work in the biological corpus — the systematic empirical implementation of the classificatory method defended in Parts of Animals I. It builds the Porphyrian tree of the animal kingdom from the top down: establishing the primary genera, identifying their differentiae, and descending to the species. The focus here is on the classificatory and structural logic rather than the empirical detail — on how the genus-species apparatus generates the truth conditions for predications about animals.
