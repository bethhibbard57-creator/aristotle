The Connections — Master Summary

All philosophical connections identified across the Aristotelian corpus reference library · in the correct philosophical order of the corpus · March 2026

The master claim: The Aristotelian corpus is a single unified argument progressing through six domains in a determinate order. The same four-position tetralemmic structure appears at every level. Aristotelian logic and Buddhist epistemology are structurally identical — not by analogy requiring paraconsistent bridging, but by identity of logical form. Every classical logical paradox arises from improper predication — from a suppressed qualification that, once restored, dissolves the paradox. The categorical classification of the biological works provides the empirical truth conditions for the entire logical structure.
The philosophical order of the corpus:
I. Logic — the Organon as instrument of all inquiry; the biological works as its empirical instantiation.
II. First Philosophy — the Metaphysics as the investigation of being qua being. Prior in dignity; posterior in our order of inquiry.
III. Natural Philosophy — Physics, On the Heavens, GC. The metaphysical principles applied to the natural world.
IV. Psychology — De Anima, Parva Naturalia. The bridge between the natural and the practical.
V. Practical Philosophy — Ethics and Politics. The individual as ethical being; the community as political being.
VI. The Arts — Rhetoric and Poetics. The logical and ethical analysis applied to persuasion and representation.

I. Logic — the Organon and the biological works as its empirical instantiation

The Organon is the instrument of all inquiry — one progressive argument from Categories through Sophistical Refutations. The biological works are not a separate domain but the empirical instantiation of the classificatory apparatus the Organon establishes. Together: the Organon gives the logical structure; the biological works give the empirical content of what makes predications true.

The Organon as unified tetralemmic architecture

Categories Ch. 2 already contains all four tetralemmic positions
Cat. Ch. 2 · De Int. Ch. 10 · Pr. An. I.46 P2
The four-fold grid of Cat. Ch. 2 generates exactly four positions: primary being (not said-of, not in) · secondary being (said-of, not in) · universal accident (said-of, in) · individual accident (not said-of, in). These are the four tetralemmic positions at the categorical level. The indefinite name (De Int. Ch. 2–3, 10) makes all four expressible as proposition forms. Pr. An. I.46 shows the indefinite name operationally essential to valid syllogistic. The tetralemmic structure is present from the first chapter of the first work and runs continuously to the last.
The Organon as a progressive argument in six stages
Categories through Sophistical Refutations P2
Categories (what is) → De Interpretatione (what is said) → Prior Analytics (how arguments are formed) → Posterior Analytics (what counts as knowledge) → Topics (how to argue from what is accepted) → Sophistical Refutations (how to detect invalid argument). Each text presupposes and extends the previous. Soph. Ref. Ch. 34 explicitly closes the project. The Rhetoric closes the argumentative loop by showing the same syllogistic structure at the level of public persuasion — the enthymeme is the syllogism from probability; rhetorical induction is the example.
The elenchus as the formal core of the entire Organon
De Int. Ch. 11 · Topics VIII · Soph. Ref. Ch. 11, 24 · Met. Γ.3–4 P1P2
The elenchus — four conditions: same subject · same predicate · same respect · same time — is the logical centre of the entire Organon. It appears in: De Int. Ch. 11 (stated); Topics VIII (practiced); Soph. Ref. Ch. 11 (as the master fallacy ignoratio elenchi); Soph. Ref. Ch. 24 (as the master dissolution); Met. Γ.3 (as the conditions of LNC). The elenchus is simultaneously a logical instrument, a dialogical practice, and an ontological principle.
Noēsis noēseōs as open awareness — the perceptual ground before words
Met. Λ.9 · DA III.4–5 · Visuddhimagga Ch. XIV, XXI · Anālayo, Satipaṭṭhāna Ch. 3 P3P4
Noēsis noēseōs is not a cognitive act in the ordinary sense — not a subject turning its attention toward an object. It is the prior condition in which both subject and object arise. The open awareness that is already present before the name, before the concept, before the distinction between knower and known. DA III.4 approaches this from the logical side: the potential nous has no form of its own — pure receptivity, pure openness. DA III.5 approaches it from the other direction: the active nous is pure actuality, pure presence, the light in which everything becomes intelligible. Open awareness is where the two meet — the prior ground in which both are already functioning before the distinction between them arises. Buddhaghosa points at this in the Visuddhimagga: in the analysis of the cognitive process (citta-vīthi) the moment of bare knowing before papañca begins — before proliferation, before naming, before categorising — is bare contact (phassa) and bare attention (manasikāra). The form is present. The awareness is present. No gap yet. Most directly: Visuddhimagga Ch. XXI's analysis of nāma-rūpa pariccheda-ñāna — the knowledge that distinguishes name and form — is the moment of seeing clearly the distinction between the bare physical event and the knowing of it before they collapse back into each other. Anālayo's Satipaṭṭhāna Ch. 3 on bare awareness is the clearest modern commentary. Lambda.9 falls silent here not because the awareness ends but because the awareness is prior to the language that would describe it. Noēsis noēseōs is the closest predication can get to pointing at open awareness before predication gives way. The name-form relationship of De Int. Ch. 1 tends toward this as its telos — the perfectly adequate name would be one where there is no gap between the symbol and what it means. Nirutti is the method of restoring the alignment; noēsis noēseōs is what the alignment is aiming at and can never reach through language. It is known only in direct contact.
Nirutti and the Sophistical Refutations — the encoding of double meaning
Soph. Ref. Ch. 4–10 · Cat. Ch. 1 · De Int. Ch. 1 · Abhidhamma nirutti · papañca P1P2P4
Nirutti — Pali for linguistic analysis, the proper tracking of words to their referents — is not merely etymology. It is the recognition that words carry multiple meanings simultaneously: the same sound encodes different referents depending on context, categorical placement, and the level of analysis at which it is being used. The Abhidhamma uses nirutti to hold all the meanings in view at once and to specify which meaning is operative in which context. This is exactly homonymy — Cat. Ch. 1. The same name, different definitions. Aristotle's opening move in the entire corpus is the recognition that words carry double or multiple meaning, and that the first task of philosophical analysis is to distinguish which meaning is operative. But nirutti goes deeper than homonymy in one respect: it recognises that the double meaning is not always a defect to be corrected. Sometimes the word encodes a genuine structural ambiguity in reality itself — the same term applies at two levels simultaneously because the two levels are genuinely related. The word is pointing at a real connection, not a mere verbal confusion. This is where nirutti and the Sophistical Refutations diverge slightly: the Sophistical Refutations treats double meaning primarily as a source of fallacy to be dissolved; nirutti treats it also as a source of insight — the double meaning is the linguistic trace of a real structural identity. The apparent contradictions — the truth gluts, the paradoxes — are not mere verbal confusions. They are linguistic traces of genuine superposition states in reality. The nirutti analysis does not simply dissolve them. It shows what they are pointing at. The secundum quid fallacy and papañca are the same error at the logical and experiential levels simultaneously — and both are dissolved by the same method: holding the double meaning in view, specifying the level, restoring the qualification.
The master dissolution: the secundum quid move dissolves every classical paradox
Soph. Ref. Ch. 10, 23 · Phys. I.8 · Phys. VI.9 · De Int. Ch. 9 · NE VII.3 P1
Every paradox suppresses a qualification — treating a qualified predication as absolute. Restoring the qualification dissolves it. The same move dissolves: the Eleatic argument against change (privation ≠ absolute non-being); Zeno's four paradoxes (potential infinity ≠ actual infinity; instantaneous position ≠ rest across an interval — the arrow is moving across the interval, the instant gives only its position not its state); the Liar; Russell's paradox; the Sea Battle; Schrödinger's Cat; the Ship of Theseus; weakness of will. One method; every classical paradox. Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 is the primary source; Physics VI.9 applies it to Zeno; NE VII.3 applies it to moral psychology.
The temporal identity condition of LNC — grounded in physics and psychology
De Int. Ch. 11 · Met. Γ.3 · Phys. IV.11–14 · On Memory Ch. 1 · Cat. Ch. 5 P1
The fourth elenchus condition — same time — is grounded by three converging analyses: Physics IV.11 (time as the number of motion with respect to before and after); On Memory Ch. 1 (the soul marks experiences as past by a temporal mark); Cat. Ch. 5 (the same subject receives contraries through time while persisting). LNC and PEM apply only to individuals with resolved temporal identity. Without memory there is no temporal identity; without temporal identity LNC cannot be properly applied.
The epistemological ascent — from perception to nous
Post. An. II.19 · DA III.3 · On Memory Ch. 2 · NE VI.6 P1P3
Post. An. II.19: perception → memory → experience → universal → nous. Nous grasps first principles by direct intellectual contact — not by inference. On Memory Ch. 2 fills in the psychological machinery: recollection is the active recovery of past images through a quasi-syllogistic associative chain. NE VI.6: nous is more accurate than scientific knowledge because it grasps what scientific knowledge proceeds from. DA III.5: the active nous is the faculty that makes this terminal grasp possible. One faculty described at the logical, psychological, and ethical levels simultaneously.

The biological works as the empirical Organon

Categorical classification gives truth values for the actual
PA I.2–5 · HA I.1 · Topics IV · Post. An. I.4 P1P4
The genus-species-differentia classification determines which predicates are per se true of which subjects. The genus is the truth-condition maker. The History of Animals is the empirical Organon: it maps the truth conditions for per se predications across the entire animal kingdom, showing at every node of the Porphyrian tree which predicates are per se at that level and which are accidental. Every paradox that involves a subject whose categorical placement is ambiguous has undetermined truth conditions — the Liar and Russell's paradox are the logical cases; monstrosities are the biological case.
The final cause is the primary explanatory principle — teleological truth conditions
PA I.4 · Physics II.8–9 · Met. Ζ.17 P1P4
The truth conditions for biological predications are teleological: a predicate is per se true of a biological subject when it belongs to the subject in virtue of its species-form — its telos. Function explains structure; structure does not explain function. This is the biological expression of Met. Zeta.17 (form as the cause of being) and the deepest connection between the biological works and the logical thesis. The species-form is the final cause; the final cause determines the per se attributes; the per se attributes are the truth conditions.
Temporal per se predication: life stages and seasons as empirical temporal identity
HA VII · HA VIII · GA V.5–8 P1
Species have different per se attributes at different seasons; individuals have different per se attributes at different life stages. This is the most extensive empirical treatment in the corpus of the temporal qualification of per se predication. The biological works give the empirical content of the temporal identity condition: truth values of biological predications are indexed to the temporal stage of the individual and the season of the species.
Nous enters from outside — the limit of the biological classification
GA II.3 · DA III.5 · Met. Λ.7 P3P4
Nous is not transmitted through the biological mechanism — it enters from outside (θύραθεν) because it has no bodily organ. The biological tree gives truth conditions for predications about the nutritive and sensitive faculties; nous is the faculty that transcends the biological classification — the faculty that grasps the classification itself. The biological analysis cannot fully determine the truth conditions of nous's own operations. This is the biological expression of the apophatic limit: nous exceeds the system it enables, as the Unmoved Mover exceeds the causal system it grounds.

II. First Philosophy — the Metaphysics as the investigation of being qua being

The Metaphysics is prior in dignity and in the order of nature — it investigates the ground of being itself. It is posterior in the order of our inquiry, which begins from what is familiar to us. First philosophy reveals what logic is about: the structure of being that makes correct predication possible.

Οὐσία and πρᾶγμα — being and doing — karma and kāraka — the deepest structural identity
De Int. Ch. 1 · Met. Λ.7–9 · Physics III.1 · Nāgārjuna MMK Ch. 8 P3P4
In De Interpretatione the chain runs: things (πράγματα — pragmata) → affections of the soul → spoken sounds → written marks. The word is πρᾶγμα — from πράσσω, to do, to act — not οὐσία. Pragma is doing; ousia is being. De Interpretatione operates at the level of the doing — the state of affairs that grounds truth values. The Categories gives the being — the fourfold categorical grid. The Metaphysics grounds the doing in the being. At the limit (Lambda.7) being and doing coincide: noēsis noēseōs, pure act without remainder. Nāgārjuna in MMK Chapter 8 analyses karma (action) and kāraka (agent) — the doing and the being — and shows that neither has svabhāva (own-nature, self-grounding presence). Each is constituted by pratītyasamutpāda — dependent origination. The truth glut — Priest's dialethia, the apparent true contradiction — is the logical image of dependent origination: a predication event caught in the middle of its own grounding, genuinely in superposition because the relational web that constitutes its presence has not yet resolved. This is not a logical anomaly requiring a new logic. It is what incomplete actuality looks like from the logical side — motion (Physics III.1) from the physical side — dependent origination from the Buddhist side. Aristotle: grounding completes in pure act — the Unmoved Mover as pure presence. Nāgārjuna: grounding never closes into self-sufficiency — śūnyatā as the open texture of presence constituted by relations. Both are right. Both know presence through dependent arising. The Unmoved Mover cannot be predicated (Lambda.9) — it is the condition of presence, not a presence among presences, known only through the dependent origination of everything that moves toward it as final cause. Three names — incomplete actuality, truth glut, śūnyatā — for the same recognition. None require a new logic. All require the restoration of the ontological apparatus. This connection will deepen as the primary texts are worked through directly.
The aporiai as dialectical space — the method of the Metaphysics is Topics VIII from the inside
Met. Β · Met. Ζ · Met. Ι · Topics VIII · Soph. Ref. Ch. 11 P2P3P4
The aporiai of the Metaphysics are not problems the argument encounters and overcomes. They are the argument's own mapping of its dialectical space — the honest acknowledgment of what must be held in tension before the investigation can proceed. Each aporia opens a space; the resolution does not close it but inhabits it. This is Topics Book VIII operating from the inside. The dialectical exchange in Topics is between two interlocutors — the questioner and the answerer. In the Metaphysics the exchange is internal — Aristotle holds both sides of each difficulty with full force before resolving it. The aporiai are not rhetorical hesitations; they are the genuine two-sidedness that makes the resolution honest rather than merely asserted. Remove the aporiai and you have conclusions without grounding. Keep them and you have philosophy. This is also the correct framework for writing the papers: each paper should maintain its dialectical space — hold the difficulty with full force before the dissolution. The reader must feel the pull of both sides before the secundum quid move resolves it. Otherwise the dissolution looks too easy and the reader does not trust it. The dialectical space is the space of genuine inquiry; the aporiai are its honest markers.
The aitiai are not derivative of being — they are the routes by which we arrive at it
Met. Α.7 · Met. Λ.7 · Physics II.3 · Post. An. II.11 P3P4
The Greek word is αἴτιον — aition — cause, explanation, that-which-is-responsible-for. It is not derived from the verb to be (εἶναι). The four aitiai are four irreducible modes of answering the question why — not four kinds of being. In Metaphysics Alpha.7 Aristotle surveys his predecessors and shows that all four causes have been recognized in some form — but only partially, applied to things and their changes, without the unified account. This is the natural-philosophical level: the four aitiai explain why things are the way they are. But by Lambda.7 the explanatory structure has become the ontological structure. The formal, efficient, and final cause converge in the Unmoved Mover; explaining why the cosmos moves as it does and explaining what sustains being itself turn out to be the same question. The aitiai begin as explanatory — four modes of answering why — and reveal themselves at their limit to be ontological. This is the hinge between natural philosophy and first philosophy, and it is what makes the four-cause structure genuinely tetralemmic rather than merely taxonomic. What Russell erased was not merely a classificatory apparatus but an explanatory-ontological one — which is why the non-classical logics, attempting to repair an explanatory gap, cannot fully succeed: the gap they are repairing is ontological.
The four tetralemmic positions of the Metaphysics
Met. Α–Γ · Δ–Η · Θ–Λ · Μ–Ν P3
Position I (Α α Β Γ): the question posed — history of causes; the aporiai as the formal tetralemmic structure of the inquiry; Gamma defending LNC as the condition of inquiry itself. Position II (Δ Ε Ζ Η): the terms determined — substance as form surviving all challenges (Zeta); actuality explaining unity (Eta). Position III (Θ Ι Κ Λ): the answer given — actuality prior to potentiality (Theta); the Unmoved Mover as pure actuality and self-thinking thought (Lambda). Position IV (Μ Ν): the competitor dissolved — the systematic elenchus of every version of the Platonic claim. Mu-Nu have no positive content of their own; they are the dissolution that leaves Lambda standing. The four positions are mutually constitutive: none stands without the others.
Lambda.7 and Lambda.9 — the apophatic limit: the text falls silent
Met. Λ.7 · Met. Λ.9 · Phys. VIII.10 · DA III.5 P3
Lambda.7: noēsis noēseōs — self-thinking thought. Lambda.9: the thinker and the thought are identical in the Unmoved Mover. This exceeds ordinary subject-predicate structure; the text cannot proceed further without distortion. Lambda.9 is the apophatic limit of the Metaphysics — and of the corpus. The same structure appears in DA III.5 (active nous: separable, unmixed, impassible, immortal) and at the limit of the Physics (VIII.10: without magnitude, moves as final cause). The text approaches the fourth position and falls silent — which is the correct response to what exceeds predication.
The Unmoved Mover = Buddhist unconditioned = unnameable Tao
Met. Λ.7 · DA III.5 · NE X.7 · EE VIII.3 P3P4
Each tradition has independently followed the same argument to its limit and arrived at the same point: the ground of all determination that is itself not determined, characterised only by negative predicates, known only by direct contact. The Unmoved Mover (Aristotle), the unconditioned asaṅkhata (Buddhism), the unnameable Tao (Daoism) are structurally identical — not by analogy across traditions but by identity of logical form. Paraconsistent logic and dialetheism (Priest) correctly identify superposition states but misname them as true contradictions rather than incomplete predication events. The correct dissolution is the secundum quid move, not the acceptance of true contradictions.

III. Natural Philosophy — the metaphysical principles applied to the natural world

The Physics, On the Heavens, and Generation and Corruption apply the metaphysical account of form, matter, actuality, and potentiality to the natural world. Natural philosophy establishes the physical ground of the logical conditions — especially the temporal identity condition — and shows the four-cause structure operating in natural change.

Motion as incomplete actuality — the physical instantiation of the catuṣkoṭi's middle positions
Phys. III.1 · Met. Θ.6 P4
Motion is the actuality of what is potentially, insofar as it is potential — incomplete actuality: the unstable intermediate between pure potentiality (prime matter, fourth position) and pure actuality (the Unmoved Mover, first position). The catuṣkoṭi's second and third positions — what is becoming, what is in both qualified senses — are physically instantiated as motion. Theta.6 distinguished complete actuality (ἐνέργεια) from process (κίνησις); Physics III.1 establishes the physical basis of that metaphysical distinction.
Time as the number of motion — the physical ground of the temporal identity condition
Phys. IV.11 · Phys. IV.13–14 · On Memory Ch. 1 P1
Time is the number of motion with respect to before and after (Phys. IV.11). The now is the same in substrate but different in position — the temporal analogue of primary being receiving contraries through time (Phys. IV.13). Time requires a counting soul (Phys. IV.14). On Memory Ch. 1: the soul marks experiences as past by a temporal mark — the psychological counterpart of the physical structure of numbered time. The physics of time and the psychology of memory are the same structure from two directions; together they ground the temporal identity condition of LNC.
The four causes as the physical tetralemma
Phys. II.3 · PA I.4 · Post. An. II.11 P4
The four causes — material, formal, efficient, final — are four irreducible modes of explanation, none reducible to any other, all four required for a complete account. This is the physical catuṣkoṭi. The same fourfold structure governs biological explanation (PA I.4) and demonstrative explanation (Post. An. II.11). The four causes operate at every level from elemental change (GC I.2) through constitutional change (Pol. V) — one causal structure, every domain.
Physics VIII and Lambda converge at the Unmoved Mover
Phys. VIII.5, 10 · Met. Λ.6–7 P3P4
The Physics approaches the Unmoved Mover from the analysis of motion; the Metaphysics approaches it from the analysis of being. They arrive at the same point: no magnitude, infinite power, eternal, moves as final cause — as the object of desire, not as an efficient cause pushing from behind. Physics VIII and Lambda are the same argument from two directions. This convergence is not coincidental but is the structural expression of the unity of the corpus: first philosophy and natural philosophy are two approaches to the same truth.

IV. Psychology — the soul as the bridge between the natural and the practical

The active nous — the same fourth position at metaphysical, biological, and ethical levels
DA III.5 · GA II.3 · Met. Λ.7 · NE X.7 P3P4
DA III.5's four properties of the active nous (separable, unmixed, impassible, eternal) are exactly the properties of the Unmoved Mover in Lambda. GA II.3 gives the biological confirmation: nous enters from outside because it has no bodily organ — it is not part of the biological causal chain. NE X.7 gives the ethical expression: the contemplative life is the human participation in what the Unmoved Mover lives completely and eternally. Three texts, one structure. The active nous is the psychological Unmoved Mover — the divine element within us.
The potential nous as the psychological analogue of prime matter
DA III.4 · Phys. I.9 · Met. Ζ.3 P4
The potential nous — no form of its own, pure potentiality for all intelligible forms — is the intellectual analogue of prime matter. Both are the fourth position of their respective domains: prime matter (neither form nor privation but the ground of both); the potential nous (neither any intelligible form nor the absence of form but the pure capacity for all). Both find their limit in pure actuality. The physical and psychological expressions of the fourth position are structurally identical.
Buddhist vijñāna and ālayavijñāna — structural convergence with DA III.4–5
DA III.4–5 · On Memory Ch. 1–2 P4
The Buddhist vijñāna (consciousness as reception of form from sense-contact) corresponds to DA III.4's potential nous. The ālayavijñāna (storehouse consciousness as the sub-personal ground of mental activity) corresponds to the active nous as the underlying productive ground. Both converge on the same logical problem: what is the ground of intellectual reception that is itself not received? Structural identity, not analogy.

V. Practical Philosophy — Ethics and Politics

The function argument as per se predication applied to human nature
NE I.7 · Post. An. I.4 · Topics IV P1P4
The function argument identifies happiness by asking what belongs to man as man — per se, not accidentally. Happiness is predicated per se of man qua rational animal; pleasure and wealth are predicated only accidentally. The Ethics' central argument is a logical argument about proper predication. The formal structure of NE I.7 applies Topics IV (what is the genus of happiness?) combined with Post. An. I.4 (what belongs per se to man?).
Equity as the secundum quid dissolution in practical reasoning
NE V.10 · Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 · Rhet. I.13 P1
Equity corrects legal justice where the universal rule fails in a particular case. The equitable person restores the qualification that the law's generality suppressed. This is the secundum quid move applied to practical reasoning: the universal law is the unqualified rule; equity is the restoration of the qualification required by the particular. The logical, ethical, and rhetorical analyses of the qualified universal are one analysis at three levels.
Phronesis and nous as the alpha and omega of practical reasoning
NE VI.5–13 · NE VI.11 · DA III.10 · Post. An. II.19 P4
Practical wisdom starts from nous (grasp of the universal end), deliberates through intermediate steps, and terminates in nous again (perception of the last particular). Nous is both the beginning and the end of practical reasoning. NE VI.11: the perception of the last particular is a form of direct intellectual contact analogous to theoretical nous. Theoretical and practical nous are the same faculty at two levels — both exceed ordinary predication and are grasped directly or not at all. This is the ethical expression of the catuṣkoṭi's fourth position in the domain of action.
The divine standard — EE VIII.3 as the explicit culmination of the practical philosophy
EE VIII.3 · NE X.7–8 · Met. Λ.7 · DA III.5 P3P4
EE VIII.3: whatever most promotes the contemplation of god is best — the only place in the ethical corpus where Aristotle states the divine standard without circumlocution. NE X.7: nous is what we most are. DA III.5: the active nous is the divine element within us. Met. Λ.7: the Unmoved Mover lives the contemplative life completely and eternally. These four texts are one claim at four levels. The Buddhist arahant living in the direct presence of the unconditioned is structurally parallel: participation in what transcends the human without ceasing to be human.
Constitutional change as political elemental transformation — the four causes in collective life
Pol. V.1–4 · GC II.2 · Physics II.3 P4
Constitutional transformation follows the same pattern as elemental transformation in GC II.2. The four causes operate in constitutional change exactly as in natural change: material cause (character of the population), formal cause (the constitutional principle), efficient cause (the faction or demagogue), final cause (the common good or its absence). Political change is the social analogue of elemental change. The political corpus is the Physics applied to collective human life.

VI. The Arts — Rhetoric and Poetics

The Rhetoric closes the argumentative loop of the Organon
Rhet. I.1–2 · Post. An. I.1 · Topics I.1 · Soph. Ref. Ch. 34 P2P4
Demonstration (Post. An.): from necessity to those who know. Dialectic (Topics): from probability to those who reason. Rhetoric: from probability and sign to any audience. The same structure — subject, predicate, middle term — at three epistemic altitudes. Improper predication generates error identically at every level. Rhetoric I.1 explicitly identifies itself as the counterpart (ἀντίστροφος) of dialectic. The Rhetoric closes the argumentative loop the Categories opened.
Recognition (ἀναγνώρισις) as the aesthetic form of the elenchus — the catuṣkoṭi enacted in life
Poetics Ch. 11, 14 · Topics VIII · De Int. Ch. 11 P1P4
Tragic recognition moves from ignorance to knowledge through the internal necessity of the plot. The elenchus moves from ignorance to knowledge through the four conditions of genuine contradiction. The same logical structure governs both. Catharsis is the emotional expression of this recognition — the purification of pity and fear through the cognitive clarification that the resolution produces. The Poetics shows the logical structure of the elenchus operating at the level of felt emotional experience. Tragedy is the catuṣkoṭi enacted in human life.
The corpus closes in the right silence
Cat. Ch. 1 · Meteor. IV.12 · Met. Λ.9 · NE X.7 P4
The corpus opens with the simplest logical distinction — homonymous vs synonymous names (Cat. Ch. 1) — and closes with the analysis showing that categorical placement gives truth values for the actual at every level of the natural world, from gold to blood to bone to flesh (Meteor. IV.12). The Unmoved Mover, the active nous, the contemplative life, and the divine standard are present throughout as the limit toward which the argument tends. The corpus as a whole instantiates the catuṣkoṭi: three positive domains and the fourth position — not silence through ignorance but silence through the completion of the argument.

Additions — the completed biological works and Meteorology

Parts of Animals II–IV — truth conditions cascade from species-form through function to structure
PA II–IV · PA I.4 · Physics II.8–9 P1P4
Every animal part is explained by its function (final cause). The truth conditions cascade: species-form → way of life → diet → digestive structure → specific organ form. The analogy principle (same function, different material realisation across genera) is the biological expression of the distinction between per se truth at the genus level and per se truth at the species level. Parts of Animals II–IV is the most sustained empirical demonstration in the corpus that categorical classification gives truth values for the actual — at the most concrete anatomical level, showing how the species-form determines which parts an animal has per se, what matter those parts require, and why they exist where they do.
Movement of Animals — practical syllogism bridges logic and biology; unmoved mover within the animal
MA Ch. 3–4, 8 · DA III.10 · Physics VIII.5 P1P4
The practical syllogism is the cognitive mechanism that triggers desire and produces bodily motion. The middle term in demonstration connects subject and predicate; the minor premise in the practical syllogism connects universal desire to particular action. These are the same logical structure at two levels. Motion is the biological conclusion of a logical argument. The joint as the fixed point within the animal — the unmoved internal support for all limb motion — corresponds structurally to the Unmoved Mover of Physics VIII.5. The same principle of a fixed ground for all motion operates at the biological and cosmological levels simultaneously.
Progression of Animals — form determines truth at the most concrete anatomical level
IA Ch. 16–19 · Physics II.9 P1P4
The direction in which the human knee bends is a per se attribute of the human species, determined by the species-form. 'The human knee bends backward' is per se true of humans because the human form requires forward bipedal locomotion and forward bipedal locomotion requires backward-bending knees. The truth condition is the species-form; the species-form is the final cause. Hypothetical necessity (Physics II.9) at the most anatomically concrete level: if the form is to be achieved, the matter must be of this kind. Form determines truth. This is the final empirical confirmation of the thesis that runs through the entire corpus.
Sleep as the biological having/not-using knowledge — On Sleep confirms NE VII.3's dissolution structure
On Sleep Ch. 1–3 · NE VII.3 · Soph. Ref. Ch. 10 P1
Sleep is the periodic suspension of the sensitive faculty — the sleeping person has the capacity for sensation but is not using it. This is the biological instantiation of the having/not-using distinction that dissolves the paradox of weakness of will in NE VII.3. The weak-willed person has knowledge but is not using it at the moment of action; the sleeping person has the sensitive faculty but is not using it. The biological and ethical cases are the same logical structure: the having/using qualification is the secundum quid qualification that resolves the apparent paradox in both domains.
Meteorology IV — the geological extension of the biological thesis: gold to blood to bone
Meteor. IV.1–12 · GC II.4–5 · PA II P1P4
Meteorology IV applies the same four-quality, two-exhalation analytical framework to geological and mineralogical stuffs (gold, iron, stone, sulphur) and organic stuffs (blood, bone, flesh, sinew). The truth conditions for predications about homoeomerous stuffs are grounded in their material constitution (ratio of qualities) and productive history (what concoction produced them). Categorical placement gives truth values for the actual at every level of the natural world — not only biological but geological and mineralogical. The corpus confirms the thesis at the deepest material level: from the purest concoction (gold, pure blood) to the least concocted (raw earth). Form determines truth across the entire range of natural substance.
