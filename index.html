The Four Papers

Paper One
Paradox dissolution via improper predication
Every classical logical paradox — Liar, Russell, Schrödinger, Sea Battle, Ship of Theseus — fails one of the four elenchus conditions of De Interpretatione Ch. 11. The dissolution is the secundum quid move: restoring the suppressed qualification. LNC and PEM apply only to individuals with resolved temporal identity.
Paper Two
The Organon as unified tetralemmic architecture
The Organon is not a collection of logical treatises but a single progressive argument. Categories Ch. 2 already contains all four tetralemma positions. The indefinite name, the elenchus conditions, and the syllogistic figures are the same structure at escalating levels of formal precision.
Paper Three
Metaphysics as recursive tetralemmic structure
The Metaphysics is a single progressive elenctic argument in four positions: the question posed (Α–Γ), the terms determined (Δ–Η), the answer given (Θ–Λ), the competitor dissolved (Μ–Ν). Lambda is the thesis that Mu-Nu defend by elenchus. The Unmoved Mover is structurally identical to Buddhist unconditioned and the unnameable Tao.
Paper Four
The complete corpus as tetralemmic structure
The same four-position structure runs from the Categories through the natural philosophy, Metaphysics, De Anima, Ethics, and Rhetoric. Aristotelian logic and Buddhist epistemology are structurally identical — not requiring Eastern-Western bridging via paraconsistent logic but by structural identity of logical form.
