The logical loop completed: from demonstration (Posterior Analytics) through dialectic (Topics) to persuasion — the enthymeme as the rhetorical syllogism, example as the rhetorical induction, and the full circuit of logos, ethos, and pathos

Click any chapter to expand. The Rhetoric closes the logical loop of the Organon. Demonstration is from true and primary premises to necessary conclusions. Dialectic is from reputable opinions to probable conclusions. Rhetoric is from what an audience will accept to persuasive conclusions. The enthymeme (rhetorical syllogism from probability or sign) and the example (rhetorical induction) are the rhetorical counterparts of the syllogism and induction of the Prior Analytics. Book I covers the kinds of rhetoric and their subjects. Book II covers the means of persuasion — ethos, pathos, and the topoi of the enthymeme. Book III covers style and arrangement.
