Aristotle · Τὰ Μετὰ τὰ Φυσικά · Tetralemmic Position IV

Metaphysics Μ (Mu) · Ν (Nu)

The limits of being: mathematical objects, the critique of Platonic Forms, the Good — the elenchus that leaves Lambda standing

The fourth and resolving tetralemmic position — the elenchus performed and the competitor dissolved. Mu and Nu do not introduce a new positive account. They perform the systematic elenchus on every form of the Platonic claim that abstract objects — numbers, Forms, the Good — are primary substances. Each version of the claim is shown to fail on its own terms. What survives the elenchus is the account of Positions I–III: being is form, form is actuality, actuality at its limit is the Unmoved Mover. The Metaphysics closes not with a new assertion but with the dissolution of the last alternative.

They are dense, technically demanding, and often read as merely polemical — but their structural role is precise: they are the fourth position of the tetralemma, the position that dissolves by showing every competitor to be self-refuting or causally inert. After Mu-Nu, only Lambda's account remains. The Metaphysics is a single progressive elenctic argument.
