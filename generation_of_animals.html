The causal analysis of how animals come to be — form transmitted through semen, matter provided by the female, the soul entering at stages, and the persistence of species through the individual's generation

Click any chapter to expand. Generation of Animals is the causal complement to the History of Animals. Where the History describes what animals are and how they live, Generation of Animals analyses how they come to be — the four causes operating in reproduction. Its central claims: (1) the male contributes the formal-efficient cause (the semen carries the form); (2) the female contributes the material cause (the menstrual blood is the matter); (3) the soul enters in stages — nutritive first, then sensitive, then (in man) rational; (4) the species is eternal because man generates man — the form is perpetuated through the temporal cycle of individual generation and death. These claims are the biological ground of the metaphysical analysis of actuality and potentiality in Zeta-Eta and Lambda.
