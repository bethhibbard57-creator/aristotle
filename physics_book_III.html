Subject: motion defined; the infinite — its existence, its mode, and its limits

Click any chapter to expand. Physics Book III contains two of Aristotle's most consequential analyses: the definition of motion as the actuality of the potential qua potential, and the account of the infinite as existing only potentially, never actually. The definition of motion is the hinge on which the entire Physics turns — everything before it (Books I–II) establishes the principles that make the definition possible; everything after (Books IV–VIII) applies and extends it. The account of the infinite resolves Zeno's paradoxes and establishes that actual infinity does not exist in nature.
