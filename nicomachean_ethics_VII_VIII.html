VII: weakness of will, pleasure and pain · VIII: friendship — its nature, kinds, and basis

Click any chapter to expand. Book VII treats three states that fall short of virtue: vice, weakness of will (ἀκρασία), and brutishness. Its central achievement is the analysis of weakness of will — how it is possible to act against one's better judgment — and its dissolution through the distinction between having knowledge and using it. Book VIII begins the two-book treatment of friendship (φιλία) — analysing its three kinds, its basis, its conditions, and its relation to justice and the political community.
