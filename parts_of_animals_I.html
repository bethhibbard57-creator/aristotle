Parts of Animals Book I — complete interactive reference · chapters 1–5

The methodological preface to the entire biological corpus — how to classify animals, why the four causes are all needed, and why the final cause is primary in biology

Click any chapter to expand. Parts of Animals Book I is the most philosophically dense text in the biological corpus. It establishes the method of biological inquiry, defends the use of genus-based classification over cross-cutting attribute-based division, shows that all four causes are required for biological explanation, and argues that the final cause — the form and function of the organism — is the primary explanatory principle. It is the logical and metaphysical apparatus of the Organon and the Physics applied explicitly to the study of living things.
