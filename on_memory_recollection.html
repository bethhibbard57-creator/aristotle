On Memory and Recollection — complete interactive reference · chapters 1–2

Memory as the retention of a perceptual image with a temporal mark · recollection as the active recovery of what is stored · the soul's experience of the past as past

Click any chapter to expand. On Memory and Recollection is the shortest and most philosophically precise of the Parva Naturalia. It is the one place in the entire corpus where Aristotle explicitly analyses how the soul marks experiences as belonging to the past — as having occurred before now. This temporal marking is the psychological ground of the temporal identity condition of LNC (De Int. Ch. 11, Met. Γ.3) and the direct link between the epistemological ascent of Post. An. II.19 and the physics of time in Phys. IV.11–14.
