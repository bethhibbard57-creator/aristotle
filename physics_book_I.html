Click any chapter to expand. Physics Book I establishes the principles of natural science: the method of inquiry (from what is familiar to us toward what is knowable in itself), the critique of the Eleatics and pre-Socratics, and the positive account of the three principles of all natural change — matter, form, and privation. This is the foundation on which all eight books of the Physics and the whole of Aristotle's natural philosophy rest.
