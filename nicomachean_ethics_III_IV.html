III: voluntary action, choice, and deliberation; courage and temperance · IV: the remaining virtues of character

Click any chapter to expand. Book III establishes the conditions under which actions are voluntary and therefore subject to praise and blame — the logical foundation of moral responsibility. It then analyses courage and temperance in detail as the first two virtues. Book IV catalogues the remaining virtues of character — liberality, magnificence, magnanimity, proper ambition, gentleness, the social virtues, and shame — completing the survey begun in Book II.
