The question of being: the history of causes, the aporia, being qua being, and the principle of non-contradiction

The first tetralemmic position — the question posed. All four books together constitute the first move of the elenctic argument: what is being, and what must any answer presuppose?

Click any chapter to expand. Position I establishes the ground of the inquiry. Alpha traces the history of causal thinking to show what any adequate account of being must include. Little Alpha defends the possibility of first philosophy against sceptical objection. Beta states the aporiai — the genuine difficulties any account must resolve. Gamma establishes being qua being as the subject and defends the principle of non-contradiction as the indemonstrable ground of all inquiry. These four books together are the elenctic opening: the question is posed in its full difficulty before any answer is attempted.
