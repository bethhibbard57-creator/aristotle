ARISTOTELIAN CORPUS — THE FOUR KINDS OF OPPOSITION

Where the distinctions between the four kinds are drawn, marked, and refined — every book, every work
CONTRADICTION
ἀντίφασις
antiphasis — affirm / deny — no middle — one must be true
e.g. he sits / he does not sit
CONTRARY
ἐναντία
enantia — maximum difference within a genus — intermediates possible
e.g. good / bad, hot / cold, pale / dark
PRIVATION / POSSESSION
στέρησις / ἕξις
steresis / hexis — lacking what naturally belongs — no intermediate — asymmetric
e.g. blindness / sight — in a creature naturally capable of sight
RELATIVE / CORRELATIVE
πρός τι
pros ti — defined by relation to another — simultaneous by nature
e.g. double / half, knowledge / the known, father / child
ENUM — Aristotle explicitly lists all four kinds BOUND — a sharp boundary is drawn between two specific kinds REFINE — a distinction within one kind is sharpened INFER — inferential difference between kinds shown APPLY — distinction presupposed and deployed in context
