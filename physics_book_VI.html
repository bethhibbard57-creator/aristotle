Physics Book VI is the mathematical heart of the Physics — a rigorous demonstration that time, magnitude, and motion are all continuous in the same sense and mutually imply each other's continuity. Every argument is tightly interlocked. The book culminates in the full dissolution of all four of Zeno's paradoxes, showing in each case that the paradox rests on a confusion between potential and actual infinity, or between the indivisible and the continuous.
