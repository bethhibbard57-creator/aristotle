Book VI on the intellectual virtues receives extended treatment — φρόνησις and νοῦς are the psychological ground of the thesis

Click any chapter to expand. Book V treats justice as the complete virtue — the virtue that is exercised toward others and encompasses all the other virtues in its scope. Book VI analyses the intellectual virtues: the five ways the soul achieves truth — scientific knowledge, craft, practical wisdom, philosophical wisdom, and nous. Practical wisdom (φρόνησις) is the master intellectual virtue for action; nous is its ground and limit. Together Books V–VI complete the bridge between the ethics of character and the account of theoretical reason that Book X will bring to its culmination.
