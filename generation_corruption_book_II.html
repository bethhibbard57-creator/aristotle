Book II applies the principles of Book I to the four elements — earth, water, air, fire — showing how they are constituted by pairs of primary qualities (hot, cold, wet, dry), how they transform into one another, and how the eternal circular motion of the heavens drives the endless cycle of generation and corruption in the sublunary world.
