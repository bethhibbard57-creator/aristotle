Physics Book V — complete interactive reference · chapters 1–6

Subject: the classification of change — its kinds, its unity, and its contrariety

Click any chapter to expand. Physics Book V applies the general definition of motion from Book III to the full taxonomy of change. It distinguishes change from motion, classifies the kinds of motion by category, defines what makes a motion one and what makes motions contrary to each other. These distinctions are the logical infrastructure that Books VI–VIII require for the analysis of continuity, infinity of motion, and the Unmoved Mover.
