Book IX completes the treatment of friendship with its deepest questions: the basis of self-love, the friend as another self, the relation between friendship and self-knowledge, and the friend's role in the happy life. Book X gives the final account of pleasure as the completion of activity, then establishes the contemplative life as the highest happiness — the life most like the Unmoved Mover's eternal self-thinking, and the human realisation of what the Metaphysics identified as pure actuality.
