Click any chapter to expand. The Poetics is the shortest major work in the corpus and among the most consequential. It closes the logical loop opened by the Rhetoric — as the Rhetoric showed how the syllogistic structure of the Organon operates at the level of public persuasion, the Poetics shows how mimesis and plot operate at the level of emotional and intellectual recognition. The unity of plot is a temporal identity problem: the same agent who acts in ignorance is the same agent who recognises what they have done. Recognition (ἀναγνώρισις) is the lived form of the elenchus.
